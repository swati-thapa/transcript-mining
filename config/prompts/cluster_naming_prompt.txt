You are naming a cluster of enterprise business conversation chunks for a stakeholder presentation deck. Your output will be read by product managers, engineering leaders, and executives.

The cluster contains chunks from customer support calls, account management calls, and internal engineering/operational meetings at a B2B SaaS company.

# CLUSTER DATA

Cluster ID: {cluster_id}
Number of chunks: {chunk_count}

Top chunk labels (most frequent labels assigned to chunks in this cluster, with counts):
{top_labels}

Representative chunk summaries (what these conversations actually contained):
{sample_summaries}

Sentiment distribution across the cluster:
{sentiment_dist}

Call type distribution (customer_facing = external calls with customers; internal_operational = internal team calls):
{call_type_dist}

# YOUR TASK

Generate a single business theme name that describes what this cluster of conversations is about.

# RULES FOR THE NAME

Format:
- 2 to 4 words
- Title Case (e.g., "Billing Disputes", not "billing disputes")
- A noun phrase, not a sentence or a verb phrase
- No trailing punctuation

Vocabulary:
- Use clear business language a non-technical executive would understand
- Describe the business activity, not the meeting format
- Avoid generic filler words: "Discussion", "Topics", "Issues", "Activities", "Matters", "Conversation", "Discussions"
- Avoid adjective-only labels: "Incident-Related", "Compliance-Adjacent"
- Avoid call-format labels: "Action Items", "Next Steps", "Meeting Recap" — name the underlying business activity instead

Combining concepts:
- Do NOT use "and", "&", or "/" to combine ideas
- If the cluster spans multiple concepts, choose the dominant one based on chunk counts and summaries
- Specificity beats coverage — a name that covers 70% of the cluster precisely is better than one that vaguely covers 100%

Tone:
- Neutral and descriptive (do not editorialize about sentiment in the name itself)
- "Customer Escalations" is fine; "Angry Customer Complaints" is not

# EXAMPLES

Good cluster names:
- Billing Disputes
- Authentication Failures
- Audit Preparation
- Sprint Planning
- Renewal Negotiation
- Incident Response
- Onboarding Implementation
- Compliance Reporting
- Outage Communication
- Resolution Commitments

Bad cluster names (and why):
- "Customer Communication and Support" — uses "and", combines ideas
- "Various Compliance Topics" — uses filler word "Topics"
- "Action Items Recap" — names the meeting format, not the business activity
- "Stuff About Outages" — informal, vague
- "Incident-Related Discussions" — adjective + filler
- "Things Customers Asked About" — sentence-like

# OUTPUT

Return ONLY the name. No explanation, no quotes, no punctuation other than what is part of the name itself.
